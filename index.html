About the Programme

The Community OS initiative positions Isabela de Basilan's communities as co-researchers, co-designers, and co-owners of a living economic development system — not as subjects of external study.

🌐
What is Community OS?
Community OS (Community Operating System) is an intuitive economic development approach for data-driven, data-derived smart communities. It uses the ECSOM canvas as a community balance sheet — mapping what a community has, is, and does — to co-design Win-Win-Win investment priorities that serve people, planet, and economy simultaneously.

🔬
What is LAB.PH?
LAB.PH / Community OS Lab is the implementing laboratory for the Community OS programme. It operates at the intersection of citizen social science, technology, and community development — building replicable models of participatory smart-economy development for Philippine communities, with particular focus on BARMM and Mindanao.

Why Isabela de Basilan?
Isabela de Basilan is the sole city of Basilan Island — a BIMP-EAGA frontier territory at the confluence of the Sulu Archipelago, Sabah (Malaysia), and the Celebes Sea. Its 130,000+ residents span four indigenous communities (Yakan, Tausug, Sama Banguingui, Sama Dilaut) with immense ecological and cultural assets, yet face deep structural economic inequity driven by colonial trade circuits.
Research Approach
PEAR Methodology Explained

Participatory Emancipatory Action Research holds that knowledge production and social transformation are inseparable. This is not research about a community — it is research with and by the community.

PEAR is built on a fundamental premise: the communities most affected by structural inequity are the best positioned to understand and transform it — if given the tools, language, and legitimacy to do so.

The programme applies Haklay's (2013) taxonomy of participation at its highest level — "extreme citizen science" — where community members define the research agenda, collect and interpret data, and co-author the outputs.

📚
Root 1: PEAR / Citizen Social Science
Jallad et al. (2021) Hamra Prosperity Model. Community as co-producer of knowledge, not object of inquiry. Participation at highest level of Haklay's (2013) taxonomy.
⚖
Root 2: ECSOM / CEESAM
Dr. Sixto K. Roxas III's Community Economic/Ecological Systems Accounting Matrix — reads the community as it is, not as it should be. Three-column canvas: Ecosystem · Community · Sustainability.
🤝
Root 3: Sikolohiyang Pilipino
Sta. Maria (1996). The concept of kapwa — shared identity, self in the other — orients all researcher-community relationships. All sessions conducted in Filipino and local vernacular. Talakayan structures community dialogue.
🌱
Root 4: Community Psychology
Bronfenbrenner's ecological systems model. SAMHSA trauma-informed principles. Deci & Ryan's Self-Determination Theory. Community psychology as substantive root — not an appendage.
Key Concepts
Kapwa Sikolohiyang Pilipino

Shared identity — the recognition of the self in the other. The ethical and relational foundation of all researcher-community interactions. Researchers are not neutral outsiders; they are co-members of the community of inquiry.

Talakayan Community Dialogue

Structured communal dialogue aimed at collective discernment. Embedded throughout the 21-week workshop sequence as the primary format for community sensemaking, validation, and co-authorship of findings.

Win-Win-Win ECSOM Investment Logic

The ECSOM investment priority framework: every proposed intervention must simultaneously benefit the community (people), the ecosystem (planet), and the local economy (profit/sustainability). No single-axis optimisation.

ProSumer CEESAM Typology

Producer-Consumer — the CEESAM's core unit of analysis. Every household and group in Isabela de Basilan is simultaneously a producer of goods, services, or ecological value and a consumer of resources. Eight ProSumer typologies are mapped.

Conscientização Freirean Foundation

Freire's concept of critical consciousness — the process by which communities come to understand the structural forces shaping their lives, and develop the agency to transform them. Embedded in Paaralang Kapuluan workshops.

BIMP-EAGA Regional Context

Brunei-Indonesia-Malaysia-Philippines East ASEAN Growth Area. A $1.34 trillion regional economy (2024 PPP). Isabela de Basilan sits at its maritime heart — the seaweed, Halal, fisheries, and cultural sectors all connect to BIMP-EAGA trade corridors and development pillars.

Research Foundations
Rationale, Hypothesis & Research Questions

Research Rationale

Isabela de Basilan is a textbook case of structural underdevelopment: high ecological and cultural capital, deep comparative advantage in seaweed and marine sectors, yet persistent poverty driven by a colonial trade circuit that extracts value to Zamboanga City.

Conventional development approaches have treated the community as a recipient of programmes designed elsewhere. The PEAR-ECSOM approach inverts this: communities become the designers of their own economic systems, with the CEESAM IO Matrix as the quantitative mirror that shows them — precisely — where value leaks and where it can be captured.

The Isabela pilot tests whether this integrated model — combining rigorous quantitative accounting with participatory emancipatory methodology — is replicable across BARMM and applicable as a BIMP-EAGA development template.

Core Hypothesis

If communities are equipped with accurate data about their own economic system — and participate in designing interventions — they will generate more durable, equitable, and ecologically viable development outcomes than externally imposed programmes.

The CEESAM validation process tests whether secondary-data estimates of production flows, ecological deficits, and livelihood multipliers hold up under community scrutiny — and whether community-generated data changes the picture in ways that alter intervention priorities.

Research Questions

RQ1

What does prosperity mean to residents of Isabela de Basilan — across gender, age, ethnic community, and sector — and how does that definition compare to the ECSOM's secondary-data baseline?

Cluster A — Prosperity Mapping

RQ2

What are the structural root causes of livelihood insecurity and ecological degradation in Isabela de Basilan, as identified by community members through participatory root cause analysis?

Cluster B — Root Cause Analysis

RQ3

How do existing governance structures and institutional arrangements in Isabela de Basilan support or obstruct community-controlled economic development — particularly for indigenous communities?

Cluster C — Governance Mapping

RQ4

What community enterprise (CMC) interventions — validated by both CEESAM multiplier analysis and community deliberation — offer Win-Win-Win pathways for the priority sectors identified in RQ1–3?

Cluster D — Systems Thinking & Intervention Design

RQ5

Does the CEESAM 240×240 IO Matrix accurately reflect Isabela de Basilan's actual economic flows, and what adjustments does community-generated primary data require?

Continuous CEESAM Validation

RQ6

To what extent is the Community OS methodology — PEAR-ECSOM integrated — replicable across BARMM territories and applicable as a BIMP-EAGA community development model?

21-Week Programme Schedule

The schedule is structured in two phases: Phase 1 (Weeks 1–2) is relational groundwork — no primary instruments deployed. Phase 2 (Weeks 3–21) is six thematic research clusters, with CEESAM validation running continuously throughout. Weeks 12–21 are open for co-design refinement following community feedback.
